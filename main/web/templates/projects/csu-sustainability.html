CSU Sustainability Initiative

The standard definition of sustainability is meeting our current needs without compromising future generations' needs. With global warming on the rise, humans collectively are not being sustainable.

In our project, we specifically look at universities' impact. Universities are small cities. For example, CSU has 30,000 students, which is larger than over 17,000 U.S. towns. An average college student accounts for 640 pounds of trash a year. Therefore, colleges have a major impact on the environment around them.

Another factor about universities to consider is that college students are the next people up in society. In a few years, these students will be the ones making decisions that affect future generations. Therefore, after being educated on the problem, these future business professionals will be better equipped to make decisions that will help the world instead of unintentionally destroying it due to ignorance.

Other universities can look to CSU as a resource when striving to become more sustainable. Colorado State University is one of the most sustainable higher education institutions in the world and was the first to receive a "platinum" rating by STARS, or the Sustainability Tracking and Rating System. CSU has now received the platinum rating three years in a row, making it the only university to achieve this feat so far.

In the video, we include steps that other universities can implement to become more sustainable, such as installing solar panels. In summary, everyone has a stake in the problem of environmental exploitation, and we need to become more sustainable to ensure humanity's future. Immediate radical change may not be feasible, but we can make progress by transforming the traditional university system, as universities should be leaders in sustainability.

The world is in our hands; it is our responsibility to help future generations.
