Browser Cookies and Privacy

Have you noticed that every time you click on a web page, a little popup appears asking you to consent to cookies? Our team was curious about browser cookie policies and how they impact users' information privacy, so we decided to research this topic.

Browser cookies are small text files that collect and store data unique to each user and the user's device. Cookies are the reason your browser can remember what you had in your online shopping cart, your login for different sites, and previously watched videos, as examples. In this sense, browser cookies are intended to enhance users' browsing experience.

While browser cookies aren't inherently dangerous, there are legal "gray areas" when it comes to how browser cookie policies are regulated. There is the potential for fraud and privacy violations, especially when users' browsers are not secured and up-to-date.

Our video discusses these challenges and strategies to improve protection against vulnerabilities associated with browser cookies.
