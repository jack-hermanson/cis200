Discriminations in the Food and Farming Industry

Discrimination in our food system is everyone's problem. It starts the second the seed is put into the ground. Black farmers make one-fifth of what white farmers make. About 24 million Americans are in food deserts; these food deserts are places that have limited access to healthy foods. Of these 24 million Americans, about 6.5 million are children. With little to no access to healthy foods, this can lead to dietary and nutritional problems and the development of health conditions, such as diabetes.

Individuals who live in these food deserts live in lower income communities, which tend to be communities with higher populations of minorities. When these individuals develop health problems, they may struggle to financially afford medical visits, tests, and treatment. We need to find a way to make healthy food more affordable and accessible to these lower income communities.
