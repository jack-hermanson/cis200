How Food Production Affects Our Environment

Introduction

Today food production is one of the biggest industries in the world. Humans produce enough food to feed over one and a half times the world population. In the US alone, thirty to forty percent of the food we produce is wasted every year. While we need food to survive, we have to look at the long-term impacts that production is having on our environment and society as a whole. Food production is putting chemicals into our rivers, sending CO2 into the air we breathe, and destroying the land that our animals live on. As the population continues to grow, so does food production. It's up to us to make a difference one step at a time before it's too late.

Soil

Problems

Soil is one of the biggest components of our ecosystem and today’s farming strategies are destroying it. The first step in farming is to make a field and the only way to do that is by clearing the land, also known as deforestation. Deforestation occurs when farmers cut down all the trees and vegetation to clear a field to farm. This causes the topsoil to dry out and the roots that hold the soil in place to die out. If crops aren’t replanted fast enough, water and wind erosion can occur causing the nutrient-rich soil to be washed away. (5) Another way today’s farming practices destroy the soil is by depleting its nutrients. Monocropping is a very popular agricultural practice where the same crop is grown over and over again on the same plot of land. This reduces the number of nutrients and organic matter from the soil. (2) Fertilizers are another huge treat to our soil. While fertilizers can increase plant growth and productivity they can also do harm. For example, nitrogen fertilizer can cause soil acidification, which can affect plant growth. Excessive fertilizer use can also cause a buildup of salts in the soil, heavy metal contamination, and accumulation of nitrate (which is a source of water pollution and also harmful to humans). (2) That’s not it, other chemical fertilizers can lead to soil degradation, nitrogen leaching, soil compaction, reduction in soil organic matter, and loss of soil carbon. (3) These issues can destroy a whole season’s harvest and cost lots of money to reverse and rebuild the soil to a healthy state.

Solutions

One of the solutions to help the soil and crops when it comes to food production is the use of organic fertilizers. Organic fertilizers offer a much more sustainable option because they are composed of all-natural elements full of living bacteria and they have rich nutrients. These bacteria and nutrients help the soil much more than man-made fertilizers that contain many harmful chemicals. (1) Another way to keep the soil healthier when producing food is through crop rotation. Crop rotation helps preserve the soil nutrients because when the same crop is planted in the same spot over and over then the specific nutrients the crop needs are used up and do not have time to replenish. Crop rotation also deters soil-borne pests from taking over and killing the harvest.(6) If organic fertilizers are not an option, another way to help the soil and crops is through biological pesticides. This is when a different insect is introduced to a crop plot and they eat or deter the other insects that are destroying the crops and damaging the soil.(7)

Water

Water Scarcity Problems

With the limited amount of freshwater that is currently left on Earth, agricultural uses of water have become excessive. With the growing population, farmers are going to need more and more water to sustain the growing agricultural business. This issue is important and can be seen by examining the 850 million undernourished people living in rural areas who depend on using large amounts of water for a livelihood in agriculture. (3). Not to mention, everyone depends on agricultural farming for their meals and nourishment. When people think about their use of water, they are thinking about the 2 to 5 liters of drinking water and 20 to 400 liters of water for household use every day. This statistic is actually false because if you count in the groceries bought from farmers, the average person’s use of water is about 2,000 and 5,000 liters per day. (3).

Solutions

This problem does not have an easy fix, but there are things that can be done to help create a change. For one, irrigation can be made more efficient. This would include ensuring there is no leakage in the irrigation systems and systems can be optimized to water only when and where water is needed. (5). Going organic can also help with water scarcity because organic fields have greater soil moisture. (5).

Water Quality Problems

In agricultural farming, many chemicals and harmful products are being used to help the process go smoothly, but this also has negative effects on the quality of our water. When farm lagoons leak, contaminated water can end up in waterways and groundwater. (4). This can become dangerous. High concentrations of nitrate in drinking water can cause methemoglobinemia, a potentially fatal disease in infants, also known as blue baby syndrome. (1). The nitrate in this drinking water comes from using high amounts of pesticides for crops. The 2000 National Water Quality Industry reported that agricultural water pollution is the leading source of water quality impacts on several rivers, lakes, and wetlands. (1).

Solutions

Some solutions to these issues that could create a positive change would be for farmers to apply fertilizers in the proper amount, at the right time of year, and using the right method. Another thing farmers could do is keep animals and their waste out of streams. (2). This will prevent nitrogen and phosphorus from getting into the water. One of the best solutions is for farmers to plant field buffers. This would require more time and money but is a great way to keep our water safe. By planting trees and shrubs around the crops, it would allow for these buffers to soak up all nutrient loss from fields and keep the chemicals out of water sources. (2).

Air

Climate Change Issues

Air pollution is very common because with the invention of more agricultural machinery, there are high rates of carbon dioxide being released into the air. This is a large component of what causes climate change, and large corporations are responsible for most of this global issue. Half of the world's habitable land is used for agriculture, which means there is a need for big machinery in order to efficiently make food (1). However, large corporations who own this land are solely focused on their profits. They do not realize or care that they contribute to climate change by emitting a great deal of CO2 into the air through their use of heavy machinery. In the same realm, agriculture altogether contributes to poor air quality because there are a lot of air pollutants including CO2 that are released into the air throughout the agricultural process (2). There are chemicals and pesticides that are used all over agricultural land that not only affects the soil, but is also released into the air. These chemicals and pesticides can help contribute to climate change as well as overall poor human health. Food production is linked to worsening air pollution, and this air pollution eventually affects the food production process (3).

Solutions

Policies and regulations to help reduce these greenhouse gas emissions must be enacted and followed in order for changes to be made and progress to be seen in terms of the environment and air quality (2). It is also up to individuals to make the real change by voting with their dollar. At the end of the day, these big corporations are primarily focused on the money aspect of it all. If more and more people are supporting environmentally friendly and sustainable companies instead of theirs, they will be forced to make a change. What you buy on an individual level sends a clear and direct message to corporations on what their customers want and what they support (4).

Livestock

Problems

Animal raising operations play a major role in our air quality and pollution. Cattle, pigs, and chickens release a huge amount of methane, nitrous oxide, and ammonia into the air that we breathe every day. These gases are progressing climate change and causing a negative impact on human health(1). Did you know that cattle are the number one agricultural source of greenhouse gases? According to Frank Mitloehner, a professor and air quality specialist in the Department of Animal Science UC Davis, each year a cow will release about 220 pounds of methane. He claims that methane from cattle is 28 times more potent than Carbon Dioxide. This problem is happening globally, for example, India has the largest cattle population in the world, but the lowest consumption of beef. So, this imposes a negative effect because it results in cows living longer and producing more methane over their lifetime (2). There must be a way to keep cattle in the farming system, while not having detrimental effects on human health.

Solutions

A way to make cows less gassy and more sustainable is to make their high-fiber diet easier to digest, so scientists have turned to feeding supplements to the cattle. One that has worked is seaweed. Ermias Kebreab, Chair Associate Dean of Global Engagement stated, “We’ve done one trial and showed that there is up to a 60 percent reduction in methane emissions by using 1 percent of seaweed in the diet.” The seaweed has been shown to not make the cow’s milk taste bad or any different, this is now being tested on beef cattle as well. This solution to the methane issue with cattle is inexpensive and reduces emissions, and ultimately bettering human health (2).
