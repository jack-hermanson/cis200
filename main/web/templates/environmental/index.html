Environmental Sustainability

The projects on this page highlight environmental sustainability challenges and solutions. Businesses are focusing on reducing their carbon footprint and overall negative impact on the environment while striving to have a positive impact on environmental sustainability.

Colorado State University is one of the most sustainable higher education institutions in the world and was the first to receive a "platinum" rating by the Sustainability Tracking and Rating System. Other universities can look to CSU as a resource when striving to become more sustainable.

CSU Sustainability

Livestock sustainability is a moral issue, with the management of livestock having impacts on ethical animal welfare and the global climate. To learn more about these topics, two teams interviewed Dr. Temple Grandin, a world-renowned animal scientist and professor at Colorado State University.

Livestock Sustainability

Over 20 billion pounds of food are wasted each year in the restaurant industry in the United States. We are addressing the issues of food waste, sustainability, and the use of locally sourced foods within the restaurant industry by proposing and designing the concept of a "greenhouse restaurant."

Restaurant Sustainability

Food production is putting chemicals into our rivers, sending CO2 into the air we breathe, and destroying the land that our animals live on. While we need food to survive, we have to look at the long-term impacts that production is having on our environment and society as a whole.
