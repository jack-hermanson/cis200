Economic Sustainability

The projects on this page highlight economic sustainability challenges and solutions. Businesses should engage in ethical practices that support their economic growth without negatively impacting other areas of sustainability.

Businesses have a unique ability to change the lives of the less fortunate people in their area. To learn more about homelessness in Colorado, our team interviewed unhoused individuals in Fort Collins.

Homelessness in Colorado

Housing is a necessity for humans, helping to fulfill the basic need for safety. However, housing prices and rents have increased substantially, often pricing low-income families out of the market.

Affordable Housing

In many communities, healthy foods are more expensive than unhealthy foods. Our project highlights the business challenge of making healthy foods affordable and accessible to all our communities.

Healthy Food & Discrimination

Online identity theft can lead to your banking accounts being used for money laundering. Our team interviewed a victim of this online fraud, a U.S. Marine Corps Major who lost thousands of dollars.

ID Theft & Money Laundering

In 2013, it was revealed that Wells Fargo employees had been engaged in a cross-selling scandal. Employees were fired for opening accounts with debit or credit cards without the customers' knowledge.

Wells Fargo Scandal

This solo project by Dylan Toohey proposes a new business idea for a company that produces and sells sustainable soft-top surfboards. His logo design combines ocean waves with a recycling sign.

Ecosurf Company Pitch
