Homelessness in Colorado

Why businesses should engage in combating homelessness

The most obvious reason that businesses should engage in combating homelessness is the ethical outcome that business can provide. Large organizations dominate cities, specifically the stores and services that line the streets. For businesses, these locations are strategic in their mission to reach the most people, but for the homeless communities of the cities, these places can provide life changing services. This is the case for one story presented in an article from Micheal Hobbes. A girl with a medical condition was saved from public humiliation and degradation by being allowed to use bathrooms within these streetside stores. Lisa Michaud, the owner of Two Big Blondes clothing store articulates, “We know it’s not the same thing as solving homelessness, but it’s important to us to get to know our homeless neighbors and offer them whatever we can”. Implementing or changing small policies such as allowing public access to bathrooms or providing free products can make a huge difference in the lives of people experiencing the homeless crisis. From large gestures to the smallest signs of compassion, businesses have a unique ability to change the lives of the less fortunate people in their area.

Why businesses would benefit from combating the problem of homelessness within their city

Beyond the ethics of serving the homeless, there are a variety of benefits towards the business themselves which offers an incentive for businesses to actively engage in incorporating philanthropic work into their business plan. The incentives that this source presents are: being able to benefit the community they are located in for higher morale, customer satisfaction, and overall image of the corporation, receiving a tax reduction for donations, and providing pride for the company through its philanthropic work. Homelessness is a relatively easy problem that businesses can address because of how many existing resources there are to donate to aand the resources businesses have, such as streetside access to bathrooms. A journal titled The Business Case for Ending Homelessness, Daniel G. Garrett describes the proven benefits of donating to the building of housing. This is a long-term solution as it provides stability that allows previously homeless people to focus their efforts on finding employment, battling addiction, mental illness, and physical health. Garrett writes, “If we all—healthcare leaders, businesses, agencies, schools, faith groups, and neighbors—join together, we will see the experience of homelessness in our communities change for everyone”. Businesses have the ability to bring us together and help those who need it, and when the incentives are in their favor it is hard to find a reason not to do it

How businesses can integrate combating homelessness into their business plan

Implementing the tool of strategic management into business plans allows for plans on how to allocate resources and money more efficiently, specifically to address the problem of homelessness in urban areas. Strategic management involves the process of setting goals and objectives for the ultimate goal of providing a competitive edge to businesses. In the past this management was mainly targeting the public sector to help homelessness, however now it seems important to focus on the business sector and allowing them to find ways to help homelessness in their area. This allows for incentives talked about above to be paired with incentives from the government.
