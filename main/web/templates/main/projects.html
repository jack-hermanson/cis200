Featured Business Sustainability Projects

This page spotlights projects selected for their creativity, focus on local business sustainability challenges, and/or primary data collection efforts.

CSU Sustainability Initiative

Colorado State University is one of the most sustainable higher education institutions in the world and was the first to receive a "platinum" rating by the Sustainability Tracking and Rating System. Other universities can look to CSU as a resource when striving to become more sustainable.

Fall 2021

Livestock Sustainability with Dr. Temple Grandin

Livestock sustainability is a moral issue, with the management of livestock having impacts on ethical animal welfare and the global climate. To learn more about these topics, two teams interviewed Dr. Temple Grandin, a world-renowned animal scientist and professor at Colorado State University.

Fall 2021

Homelessness in Colorado

Businesses have a unique ability to change the lives of the less fortunate people in their area. To learn more about homelessness in Colorado, our team interviewed unhoused individuals in Fort Collins. Our page discusses ways businesses can help fellow neighbors who need housing in their local communities.

Fall 2021

Affordable Housing

Housing is a necessity for humans, helping to fulfill the basic need for safety. However, housing prices and rents have increased substantially, often pricing low-income families out of the market. Our project explores affordable housing, with a focus on Colorado.

Fall 2021

Sustainable Restaurants

The “Greenhouse Restaurant” concept is a locally sourced fine dining restaurant. All the food that is used in the restaurant comes from these greenhouses and the food waste generated in the restaurant will be composted and used again in the soil. This concept will help to address the issues of sustainability, food waste, and locally sourced food in the restaurant industry.

Fall 2021

Healthy Food and Discrimination

In many communities, healthy foods are more expensive than unhealthy foods. Our project highlights the business challenge of making healthy foods affordable and accessible to all our communities.

Fall 2021

Social Isolation with Dr. Jennifer Harman

To provide a closer look at something we all have faced during the COVID-19 pandemic, we interviewed a renowned professor at Colorado State University, Dr. Jennifer Harman, about social isolation.

Fall 2021

Online Identity Theft and Money Laundering

Online identity theft can lead to your banking accounts being used for money laundering. Our team interviewed a victim of this online fraud, a U.S. Marine Corps Major who lost thousands of dollars.
