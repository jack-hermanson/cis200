Social Isolation

In an effort to provide a closer look at social isolation during the COVID-19 pandemic, we interviewed a renowned professor of Colorado State University, Dr. Jennifer Harman.

Social isolation can be defined structurally as the absence of social interactions, contacts, and relationships with family and friends, with neighbors on an individual level, and with "society at large" on a broader level. Social isolation has become a common problem in today's society.

When COVID-19 was at its peak, interactions with people outside one's home was severely limited. With these limited interactions, many people experienced feelings of depression and loneliness, negatively impacting people's mental health.

Social isolation heightens risks for various health concerns and can affect anyone at any age. Given the grave consequences that can result from social isolation, our team reached out to an expert on this topic, Dr. Jennifer Harman, a psychology professor at CSU. She bluntly explains that the risks and consequences of social isolation can be severe, especially when social isolation is experienced over a prolonged time. She also offers advice and solutions to overcome social isolation and feel connected again.
