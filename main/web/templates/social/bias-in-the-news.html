Follow Up

Keeping in mind what our expert had to say in the interview, we have compiled a few different ways to keep your news feed in check:

Sourcing

Knowing how the information was sourced can be a good indicator on the quality of the news being reported. Our expert suggests that information could be more credible from a named source rather than an anonymous source. It seems to be the case that people ask for anonymity when they want to insult or smear someone, rather than making a statement and backing it with your name. When you are reading news about COVID-19 the information should be sourced from an expert in that field.

Authenticity

Another way to judge the quality of the piece is through the authenticity of the reporter, subject, or expert. Is the piece backed by someone who has a history of cherry-picking evidence to support a certain narrative, or someone who has a history of reporting all of the facts in the story? Keeping in mind this potential bias is an important way you can keep your news feed in check.

Understand the Target Market

Understanding the target market for the media source. It is beneficial to listen or read many news sources, but it is important to understand that each source might tend to lean in a certain way. Knowing that Fox news might likely lean toward a conservative narrative, or that CNN could often lean toward a liberal narrative is a good way to listen to multiple versions of the same story in order to get all of the facts.

Takeaway

The odds of getting completely unbiased news pieces are slim to none. Thankfully, using the above tips you can help keep your news feed as healthy as possible. Remember to check how the information was sourced, the authenticity of the reporter or expert, and understanding a news sources possible bias.
