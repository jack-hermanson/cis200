Marketing

The Marketing team created several designs for the website, including logo designs, color palette, and font selections. The team used a variety of software (e.g., Adobe Photoshop, GIMP) to create the designs. Using Qualtrics, the team surveyed the two sections of CIS200 to determine the most preferred designs. After analyzing the results of the survey, the Marketing team shared the top website and logo designs with the IS team for implementation. The Marketing team began planning the external marketing campaign using creative briefs and launching an Instagram page for the website. They also met with the Marketing Director of CSU’s College of Business to learn professional strategies and policy constraints related to publishing and marketing the website.

A Letter to the Next Marketing Team

As a whole, the Marketing team agrees that this project was a great overall experience. It taught us how to work together as a team, accomplish goals with a proper deadline and timeline, and gain overall experience in what marketing a website could potentially look like.

Working with strangers was tough at first. Communication is everything, and we did struggle with that. For the next Marketing team, we suggest getting into contact with everybody on your team as soon as possible so everyone can stay in the loop. Also, talk to other groups. Stay connected with one another, share your ideas and ask questions; this will only benefit your team's work and the website as a whole.

Your goal with this project is to work together effectively and efficiently. It is a great way to gain experience in your career field since almost everyone will be working with a team. Take this project seriously. The more you and your team work together to the best of your abilities, the more it should be an easy, fun learning experience.

Due to this project being the first for Dr. Serrano and her students, we had plenty more to finish. You will have a more thought-out schedule and approach. With that being said, we suggest using creative briefs and/or other layouts to help get you started and help you and your team decide on what you want your marketing experience to be like. Remember, this is meant to benefit you, so have fun with it!

— The Marketing Team, 2021
